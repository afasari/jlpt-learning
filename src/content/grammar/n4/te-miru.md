## Meaning
"Try doing [X]" - Used when you want to try something to see how it goes or to experience something.

## Usage
Verb-te form + みる

## Examples
- **この料理を食べてみます。**
  (Kono ryōri o tabetemasu.)
  I'll try eating this dish.

- **新しい店に行ってみたいです。**
  (Atarashii mise ni itte mitai desu.)
  I want to try going to the new store.

- **日本語で話してみてください。**
  (Nihongo de hanashite mite kudasai.)
  Please try speaking in Japanese.

## Notes
- Conjugate the verb to its te-form and add "miru"
- "Miru" is usually used in its dictionary form ("mitai" for want to try) or masu form ("mimasu")
- This grammar point is distinct from just "te-iru" as it specifically implies trying/experiencing
